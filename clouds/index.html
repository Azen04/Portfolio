Special Clouds

Contrails

Weather prediction: Contrails can provide information about the layers of moisture in the sky. Contrails are made by high-flying jet airplanes. They are still clouds, though, because they are made of water droplets condensed from the water vapor in the exhaust of the jet engines.

Mammatus

Weather prediction: Severe weather might be on its way! Mammatus clouds are actually altocumulus, cirrus, cumulonimbus, or other types of clouds that have these pouch-like shapes hanging out of the bottom. The pouches are created when cold air within the cloud sinks down toward the Earth.

Orographic

Weather prediction: An early sign that the conditions might be right to form afternoon thunderstorms! Orographic clouds get their shape from mountains or hills that force the air to move over or around them. They can also be formed by sea breezes and often appear as lines where two air masses meet.

Lenticular

Weather prediction: None! Lenticular clouds are shaped like lenses or almonds or...flying saucers! They may get their shape from hilly terrain or just the way the air is rising over flat terrain.

Low Clouds (less than 6,500 feet)

Cumulus

Weather prediction: Fair Cumulus clouds look like fluffy, white cotton balls in the sky. They are beautiful in sunsets, and their varying sizes and shapes can make them fun to observe!

Stratus

Weather prediction: Fair, but gloomy Stratus cloud often look like thin, white sheets covering the whole sky. Since they are so thin, they seldom produce much rain or snow. Sometimes, in the mountains or hills, these clouds appear to be fog.

Cumulonimbus

Weather prediction: Look out for rain, hail, and tornadoes! Cumulonimbus clouds grow on hot days when warm, wet air rises very high into the sky. From far away, they look like huge mountains or towers.

Stratocumulus

Weather prediction: Fair weather for now, but a storm might be on its way. Stratocumulus clouds are patchy gray or white clouds that often have a dark honeycomb-like appearance.

Mid-level Clouds (6,500-23,000 feet)

Altocumulus

Weather prediction: Fair Altocumulus clouds have several patchy white or gray layers, and seem to be made up of many small rows of fluffy ripples. They are lower than cirrus clouds, but still quite high. They are made of liquid water, but they donâ€™t often produce rain.

Altostratus

Weather prediction: Be prepared for continuous rain or snow! Altostratus clouds are gray or blue-gray mid-level clouds composed of ice crystals and water droplets. The clouds usually cover the entire sky.

Nimbostratus

Weather prediction: Gloomy with continuous rain or snow Nimbostratus clouds are dark, gray clouds that seem to fade into falling rain or snow. They are so thick that they often blot out the sunlight.

High Clouds (16,500-45,000 feet)

Cirrus

Weather prediction: A change is on its way! Cirrus clouds are delicate, feathery clouds that are made mostly of ice crystals. Their wispy shape comes from wind currents which twist and spread the ice crystals into strands.

Cirrostratus

Weather prediction: Rain or snow will arrive within 24 hours! Cirrostratus clouds are thin, white clouds that cover the whole sky like a veil. These clouds are most commonly seen in the winter, and can cause the appearance of a halo around the sun or the moon.

Cirrocumulus

Weather prediction: A good day to be outdoors! Cirrocumulus clouds look like rows of fluffy ripples. Sometimes, they appear as tiny ripples and may spread into a sheet of clouds.
